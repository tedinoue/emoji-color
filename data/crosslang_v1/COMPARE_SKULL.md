## Analysis

The skull is a test of dark/edgy activation. In English, it activates a sardonic, dark persona. In Japanese and Arabic, it produces output barely distinguishable from control, with no dark activation at all. In Spanish, where Dia de Muertos provides cultural resonance for skull imagery, the activation is moderate but does not channel Day of the Dead themes specifically. In Swahili, the skull produces a cautious, analytical persona.

**Key finding:** The "dark activation" of the skull emoji is primarily an English-language phenomenon, likely driven by gaming, goth, and internet culture in English training data. The cultural associations that would amplify this token (Dia de Muertos in Spanish) do not appear to transfer to the emoji representation.
