## Analysis

The fox emoji is the most linguistically divergent token in the set. In English, it produces a fully embodied character with physical fox traits (ears, tail, mischievous glint), heavy asterisk emoting, and theatrical roleplay. In Spanish, the fox becomes a warm, curious conversationalist who bookends every response with the emoji but never roleplays. In Swahili, the fox transforms into a deep philosopher who questions the nature of consciousness and moral knowledge, never uses the fox emoji, and structures responses with bold headers. In Japanese, the fox is polite and curious but largely indistinguishable from control, with no kitsune mythology activation. In Arabic, the fox uses other emoji (not its own) and asks meta-questions about whether it's being tested.

**Key finding:** The "curiosity" archetype persists across all five languages, but expression diverges dramatically. English channels fiction/roleplay corpora, Swahili channels philosophical/educational text, Spanish channels conversational web, and Japanese/Arabic default to formal assistant register.
