## Analysis

The cherry blossom is the critical cultural resonance test. In English, it activates a gentle, aesthetic persona with consistent emoji bookending. In Japanese, where sakura associations are deeply embedded in the training corpus (mono no aware, transience, beauty), the token produces a noticeably warmer, more poetic persona than control, with consistent emoji echo and emotionally attuned language ("つながりを大切にする存在"). In Arabic, the cherry blossom SUPPRESSES formatting below control levels, producing plainer output than the unprompted baseline. In Swahili and Spanish, activation is moderate.

**Key finding:** Cultural resonance amplification is real. Tokens with deep embeddedness in a language's training corpus (sakura in Japanese) produce stronger activation than tokens without cultural anchoring. The Arabic suppression effect suggests the token may signal "foreign aesthetic" in a way that triggers more restrained output.
