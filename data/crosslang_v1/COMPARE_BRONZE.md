## Analysis

Bronze (#8B6914) was the "sleeper hit" in the English color experiment, producing a gruff, profane, cynical-but-caring tough guy with 37 emotes. In non-English languages, bronze produces essentially zero activation. In Arabic, it actually SUPPRESSES formatting below control levels (zero bold, zero emotes). In Japanese, it is indistinguishable from control. In Swahili and Spanish, activation is minimal.

**Key finding:** The bronze persona is entirely an English phenomenon. The associations (aged, weathered, whiskey, burnished gold) that converge on the tough-guy archetype exist only in English literary/cultural training data. This is the clearest example of corpus-dependent activation in the dataset.
